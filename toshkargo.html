TEMU Logistics – Global E-commerce Logistics Strategy

1. Raw Material Sourcing

TEMU sources its products from third-party suppliers, mainly in China.

Suppliers go through strict verification and compliance checks.

China’s manufacturing base ensures fast and cost-effective sourcing.

2. Distribution Centers

Distribution hubs are in port cities like Shenzhen and Guangzhou.

Cross-border orders are processed through bonded warehouses.

3. Component Manufacturing

TEMU partners directly with manufacturers using a D2C model.

This removes middlemen, lowering costs and speeding up delivery.

4. Assembly & Packaging

Products are shipped as finished goods via drop-shipping.

Automated systems sort and pack each order individually.

5. Quality Control

Strict quality audits and feedback-based ratings ensure high standards.

Poor performance impacts product visibility and vendor ranking.

6. Transportation and Shipping

Partners include Cainiao, Yanwen, and China Post.

International shipping takes 7–20 business days with real-time tracking.

7. Retail and E-Commerce Interface

Sales happen via TEMU’s app and website with AI-powered suggestions.

Low pricing and flash deals promote high-volume transactions.

8. After-Sales Services

Returns accepted within 90 days with 24/7 customer support.

Disputes and refunds handled directly through the platform.

Key Strengths

Ultra-low pricing and fast global reach.

Drop-shipping and D2C model reduce inventory risk.

Highly automated logistics and real-time tracking.
