Design Thinking - Space Delivery Game

Created by Shakhzodbek and Jakhongir

Step 1: Empathize

Who is experiencing the problem? People who enjoy space games but want them to be quick and strategic.

What are their needs and goals? They want to play short, brain-using games that are fun and challenging.

What challenges do they face? Long or boring games, confusing instructions, lack of feedback.

Emotions involved: Excitement, stress, joy.

Success looks like: Winning with the best route, getting stars, enjoying a smooth experience.

Step 2: Define

Problem Statement:
Strategy game players need a fun and fast way to plan space routes because they enjoy brain challenges without wasting too much time.

Step 3: Ideate

  • Add route planning with drag-and-drop
  • Show energy cost in real time
  • Star ratings based on route efficiency
  • Ship animation during travel
  • Multiple themes: space, robot, magic
  • Bonus stars for secret planets
  • Leaderboard to compare with friends

Step 4: Prototype

- A map with 6 star systems

- Player taps systems to build route

- Energy cost and path shown

- Rearrangement allowed before confirming

- Ship flies on click, score and rating shown

Step 5: Test

What did testers like? Energy counter and space visuals

Questions they had: Undo option? Easy restart?

What they suggested: Add levels, sound, hidden stars

Improvements made: Undo button, music, new maps
